Northwestern Percussion Dictionary

Northwestern (NU) students love shortening phrases! Here are some of the percussion studio's favorites.

Jeffrey Ryan 5-26-2022

  2. Events
  3. Ensembles

Locations ↑
  • Reg (Regenstien hall)
    - The old section of the main music building. You can find most of our practice rooms, 121, 007, 011, and reg lounge here.
  • 121
    - Main percussion room.
  • 007 (“double-o-seven” or “o-seven”)
    - Main percussion storage room
  • 011 (“o-eleven”)
    - Large ensemble rehearsal room in Reg basement. Contains the 011 cage which holds many percussion instruments.
  • Reg Lounge
    - Near 121, a lounge open to students. Great for chatting, naps, quick meals, and last minute homework.
  • RMCA (Ryan Center for the Musical Arts)
    - Newer section of the music building. You can find most music classrooms, MCR, and Galvin here.
  • MCR (Master Class Room)
    - Medium sized hall used for studio class and recitals.
  • Galvin (Galvin Hall)
    - Newest hall in the music school, just to the right of RCMA's main entrance. Used for upscale chamber concerts and recitals.
  • Pick (Pick Staiger Concert Hall)
    - Large ensemble performance hall, separated from Reg and RCMA.
  • PRR (Pick Rehearsal Room)
    - Large ensemble rehearsal space in Pick.
  • Pick Stage
    - Performance stage in Pick.
  • Pick Cage
    - Storage closet for percussion instruments in Pick, connected to PRR.

Events ↑
  • Pools (Pool auditions)
    - Before each quarter, all studio members play through excerpts for a blind pannel, usually just Prof Wu. Prof Wu uses your pools performance and existing knowledge of your playing to decide your ensemble for the quarter.
  • Pools pools
    - Where studio members bet on which excerpts will be called during the pool audition. Usually, the worst guesser must buy the best guesser ice cream or boba.
  • Studio
    - Studio class is held each Wednesday at noon in MCR. You can perform anything from an exercise from Level I to a full solo or chamber piece. Typically, Prof Wu and studio members offer feedback on the performance. Playing in studio massively decreases performance nerves and improves your musicianship, so take advantage of this early on!
  • Studio dinner
    - Towards the end of each quarter, Prof Wu takes everyone in the studio to enjoy a meal together. The atmosphere is very casual.

Ensembles ↑
  • SWE (Symphonic Wind Ensemble-"swee")
    - The upper level wind band, directed by Dr. Mallory Thompson (Dr. T). Usually 5-6 percussionists. Rehearses MWF afternoons.
  • NUSO (NU Symphony Orchestra-"new-so")
    - The upper level symphony orchestra. Usually 4-5 percussionists. Rehearses MWF afternoons.
  • Sym Band or SB (Symphonic band-"sim-band")
    - The mid-level wind band, frequently where first years are placed, directed by Dr. Sean Vondran. Usually 5-6 percussionists. Rehearses TT afternoons.
  • NUCO (NU Chamber Orchestra-"new-co")
    - The mid-level symphony orchestra, frequently where first years are placed, directed by Dr. Robert G. Hasty. Usually 1-3 percussionists. Rehearses TT Afternoons.
  • CME (Contemporary Music Ensemble-"C. M. E")
    - Chamber group which plays new music. Usually 2 percussionists. Rehearses MWF mornings.
  • Opera
    - Opera theater. Usually 2 percussionists.
  • NUPE (NU Percussion Ensemble-"newp")
    - Directed by Prof Wu. Everybody in the studio participates! Typically rehearses TW evenings.
  • Concert Band
    - Concert band usually made up of non-music majors.
  • Philharmonia
    - Symphony orchestra usually made up of non-music majors.
